Design principles

Three ideas drive every decision below

The subject matter — reading people for flight risk — sets a higher bar than a normal dashboard. The look has to earn trust.

🌿

Humane, not clinical

The cool cyan-and-slate brand stays calm through soft shapes, low-contrast surfaces, and generous space — so it feels supportive, not like a dense surveillance console.

📈

Signal, not alarm

Muted, desaturated semantics. We show trend and direction — sparklines, arrows, soft badges — so a reading invites a conversation rather than flagging a person.

⚡

On-brand & quiet

Lightspeed cyan carries energy and action; slate carries everything calm and structural. The bright cyan is a spotlight — used sparingly, never wallpaper.

Color

Palette — Lightspeed cyan + slate

Sampled from the logo. Cyan splits into a bright brand cyan (the mark, accents, active states) and a deeper cyan for buttons and links so white text stays readable.

Navigation

Recommendation: left sidebar, not top tabs

A left vertical sidebar for primary destinations, a thin top bar for global actions, and in-page tabs for sub-views within a surface.

Why sidebar wins for this product

Four surfaces today — Home · My Week · Organization · Admin — but Organization, Admin, and later exec analytics all grow sub-sections. A sidebar absorbs that growth; top tabs run out of room around 5–7 items and can't group or nest. It's also the category convention (15Five, Lattice, Culture Amp, Linear, Asana), and it preserves vertical height for org trees and people grids.

✓ Left sidebar

  • Scales to more surfaces + nested sections
  • Matches every comparable people-product
  • Holds workspace switcher, role context, separated Admin
  • Preserves vertical space for tables & org trees
  • Collapses to an icon rail for dense screens

Top tabs alone

  • Caps out ~5–7 items, no nesting
  • Sub-tabs eat vertical room
  • Weak home for an Admin area
  • Only wins for a permanently flat, shallow app
  • Still useful — as in-page tabs within a surface
