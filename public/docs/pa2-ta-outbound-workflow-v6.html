Where we started

PA-2 was built as a requisition-centric hiring tool — a manager says “I need a hire,” we post a job, screen applicants, interview, decide. That's the same pipeline every ATS on the market optimizes. It works, but it doesn't compound, and it doesn't ask the harder question: should we even be hiring for this?

Where we landed

A capability-centered system built on two products and one shared foundation. The first product identifies what’s actually missing. The second finds and proves the right person. The foundation — a single capability model authored from ST6’s judgment — connects everything and makes it defensible.

Compass — Demand & Gap Intelligence
“What do we actually need?”

Before posting a job, ask: is there a real capability gap? Is hiring the right way to close it — or should we train, redeploy, or restructure?

The unit is the gap, not the requisition
Hiring is one closure path among several
AI diagnoses whether it's a hiring, training, or process gap
Lens — Talent Evaluation Engine
“How do we find and prove it?”

Once we know we're hiring: continuous sourcing, observed-work evaluation, structured interviews, and evidence-based decisions.

Observed work at the center, not resume proxies
AI sources, scores, and manages logistics at scale
Humans own interviews, references, and final judgment
Shared Foundation
— The Capability Model

One explicit model of what “good” looks like — authored from ST6's judgment, projected into every surface. Sourcing scans for it. Work trials test for it. Interviews probe it. Performance monitoring measures whether it predicted correctly. Both layers inherit the same model. That's what makes the parts multiply rather than add.

“Build on Compass's ontology. Ship Lens first.”

The schema is gap-rooted from day one, but Lens ships first — it serves Solovis and ST6 now, and it's where the pain lives. Gap diagnosis initially resolves to “hire” almost every time, because it's the only closure path wired up. The others light up as sister modules mature.
